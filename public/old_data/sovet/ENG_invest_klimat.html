The Council for Improving the Investment Climate in the Kursk region was established in order to attract investment and stimulate economic development. It is an advisory body composed of representatives of business, government agencies, scientific and public organizations.

The main tasks of the Council for improving the investment climate include:

  1. Analysis of the investment climate in the region and identification of problems hindering investment attraction.
  2. Development of recommendations to improve the investment climate, including proposals to amend legislation, reduce administrative barriers, develop infrastructure, etc.
  3. Interaction with investors, providing them with information about investment opportunities in the region, assistance in solving emerging problems.
  4. Monitoring the implementation of measures to improve the investment climate and evaluating their effectiveness.
  5. Organization of events aimed at attracting investments, such as investment forums, exhibitions, project presentations, etc.

The Council for Improving the Investment Climate plays an important role in the development of the region, as it helps to create favorable conditions for attracting investment, which contributes to the creation of new jobs, infrastructure development and improving the standard of living of the population.
