The investment Declaration of the Kursk Region is a document that defines the basic principles and directions of the investment policy of the region. It represents a set of measures aimed at creating a favorable investment climate and attracting investments into the regional economy.

The investment declaration of the Kursk region contains the following sections:

  • Goals and objectives of the investment policy.
  • The main directions of investment policy.
  • Priority sectors of the economy for investment.
  • Investor support mechanisms.
  • Conditions and procedure for the provision of state support.
  • Guarantees to investors.

The investment Declaration of the Kursk Region is an important tool for attracting investments to the region and creating a favorable investment climate. It defines the basic principles and directions of investment policy, as well as provides investors with guarantees and support measures

The Kursk region is located in the center of the European part of Russia, which makes it an advantageous place for the development of various sectors of the economy. It is located at the intersection of important highways, which provides convenient access to other regions of the country and foreign markets. In addition, the Kursk region has favorable climatic conditions for agriculture and industry.

The region has a high energy supply and reserves of electric power capacity due to the operation of the Kursk NPP (with a capacity of 4000 MW), located 40 km from the city of Kursk. 6 main gas pipelines with a length of 1.2 thousand km pass through the territory of the Kursk region, as well as gas pipelines with a length of 0.6 thousand km .

The Kursk Region has a developed network of railways and highways, including three major railway junctions and federal highways M-2 and A-144. Air traffic is carried out through Kursk airport.

Small and medium-sized businesses represent a diversified sector of the economy and act as a strategic resource for the development of the region's territory. More than 120 thousand people work in this sector of the economy, which is about 25% of the total number of people employed in the region's economy.

The Kursk region has a rich mineral resource potential, consisting of unique deposits of iron ores, rare and non-ferrous metals. Iron ores are concentrated in more than 30 deposits (Kursk magnetic anomaly, the world's largest area in terms of iron reserves). The Mikhailovsky Mining and Processing Potential, Russia's second largest iron ore mining and processing complex, operates in the region.

The Kursk region has a rich historical and cultural heritage, which makes it attractive for tourists. There are many attractions in the region, including museums, architectural monuments and nature reserves. Investments in tourism can lead to the creation of new jobs and an increase in the region's income.
